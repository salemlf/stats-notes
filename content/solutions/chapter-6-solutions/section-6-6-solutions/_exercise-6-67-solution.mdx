a) Let $h(y_1, y_2) = (h_1(y_1, y_2), h_2(y_1, y_2)) = (y_1 / y_2, y_2) = (u_1, u_2)$.
Note that this is a one-to-one transformation because

$$
\begin{align*}
    (v_1 / v_2, v_2) = (w_1 / w_2, w_2) & \Longleftrightarrow ((v_1 / v_2) \cdot v_2, v_2) = ((w_1 / w_2) \cdot v_2, w_2)\\
    & \Longleftrightarrow (v_1, v_2) = ((w_1 / w_2) \cdot w_2, w_2)\\
    & \Longleftrightarrow (v_1, v_2) = (w_1, w_2).
\end{align*}
$$

Then the inverse will be $h^{-1}(u_1, u_2) = (h_1^{-1}(u_1, u_2), h_2^{-1}(u_1, u_2)) = (u_1 u_2, u_2) = ((y_1 / y_2) \cdot y_2, y_2) = (y_1, y_2)$, making

$$
J = \det
\begin{bmatrix}
    u_2 & u_1\\
    0 & 1
\end{bmatrix} = (u_2)(1) - (u_1)(0) = u_2.
$$

Thus,

$$
f_{U_1, U_2}(u_1, u_2) = f_{Y_1, Y_2}(h_1^{-1}(u_1, u_2), h_2^{-1}(u_1, u_2)) |J| = f_{Y_1, Y_2}(u_1 u_2, u_2) |u_2|.
$$

---

b)

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{U_1}(u_1) & = \int_{u_2 = -\infty}^{u_2 = \infty} f_{U_1, U_2}(u_1, u_2) du_2\\
    & = \int_{u_2 = -\infty}^{u_2 = \infty} f_{Y_1, Y_2}(u_1 u_2, u_2) |u_2| du_2 \quad (\text{by part (a)})
\end{align*}
$$

---

c)

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{U_1}(u_1) & = \int_{u_2 = -\infty}^{u_2 = \infty} f_{Y_1, Y_2}(u_1 u_2, u_2) |u_2| du_2 \quad (\text{by part (b)})\\
    & = \int_{u_2 = -\infty}^{u_2 = \infty} f_{Y_1}(u_1 u_2) f_{Y_2}(u_2) |u_2| du_2 \quad (\text{by independence and Theorem 5.4})
\end{align*}
$$
