Since $W = Y_1 + Y_2$ and $Y_1$ and $Y_2$ are independent, then

$$
\begin{align*}
    m_W(t) & = m_{Y_1}(t) m_{Y_2}(t)\\
    \implies m_{Y_2}(t) & = \frac{m_W(t)}{m_{Y_1}(t)}\\
    & = \frac{(1 - 2t)^{-\nu / 2}}{(1 - 2t)^{-\nu_1 / 2}}\\
    & = (1 - 2t)^{-(\nu - \nu_1) / 2}.
\end{align*}
$$

This is the moment-generating function for a $\chi^2$ distribution with $\nu - \nu_1$ degrees of freedom.
