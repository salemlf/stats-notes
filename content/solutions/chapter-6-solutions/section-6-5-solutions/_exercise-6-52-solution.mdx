a) Letting $U = Y_1 + Y_2$, the moment-generating function of $U$ will be

$$
\begin{align*}
    m_U(t) & = m_{Y_1}(t) m_{Y_2}(t)\\
    & = \exp \left[\lambda_1 (e^t - 1) \right] \exp \left[\lambda_2 (e^t - 1) \right]\\
    & = \exp \left[(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2) (e^t - 1) \right].
\end{align*}
$$

This is exactly the moment-generating function for a Poisson distribution with mean $\lambda_1 + \lambda_2$.
