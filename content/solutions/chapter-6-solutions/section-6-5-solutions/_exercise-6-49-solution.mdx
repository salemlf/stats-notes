By <a href="/docs/chapter-6/section-6-5#thm-6-2" target="_blank">Theorem 6.2</a>, the moment-generating function of $U = Y_1 + Y_2$ is

$$
\begin{align*}
    m_{U}(t) & = m_{Y_1}(t) m_{Y_2}(t)\\
    & = [pe^t + (1 - p)]^{n_1} [pe^t + (1 - p)]^{n_2}\\
    & = [pe^t + (1 - p)]^{n_1 + n_2}.
\end{align*}
$$

This is the moment-generating function for a binomial distribution with parameters $n_1 + n_2$ and $p$.
Thus, the probability function for $U = Y_1 + Y_2$ is

$$
p_U(u) = \binom{n_1 + n_2}{u} p^{u} (1 - p)^{(n_1 + n_2) - u}, \quad u = 0, 1, 2, ..., n_1 + n_2.
$$
