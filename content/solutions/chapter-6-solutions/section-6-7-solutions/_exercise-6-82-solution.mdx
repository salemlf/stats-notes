Letting $F(y)$ be the common distribution function, $F(m) = 1 - e^{-m / \beta} = \frac{1}{2} \Longleftrightarrow m = \beta \ln(2)$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    1 - [F(\beta \ln(2))]^n & = 1 - \left(1 - e^{-\beta \ln(2) / \beta} \right)^n\\
    & = 1 - \left(1 - e^{\ln(1 / 2)} \right)^n\\
    & = 1 - \left(1 - \frac{1}{2} \right)^n\\
    & = 1 - \left(\frac{1}{2} \right)^n.
\end{align*}
$$
