a) The cheaper stock price will be represented by $Y_{(1)}$, and to find the density function for this we will need to find the common distribution function $F(y)$ of $Y_1$ and $Y_2$:

$$
F(y) =
\begin{cases}
    0, & y < 4,\\
    \int_{t = 4}^{t = y} \frac{1}{2} e^{(-t / 2) + (4 / 2)} dt\\
    = \int_{t = 4}^{t = y} \frac{1}{2} e^{2 - t / 2} dt\\
    = -e^{2 - t / 2} \biggr|_{t = 4}^{t = y}\\
    = -e^{2 - (y) / 2} - (-e^{2 - (4) / 2})\\
    = 1 - e^{2 - y / 2}, & 4 \leq y.
\end{cases}
$$

Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_{Y_{(1)}}(y_1) & = 2[1 - F(y_1)]^{2 - 1} f(y_1)\\
    & = 2 \left[1 -
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y_1 < 4,\\
        1 - e^{2 - y_1 / 2}, & 4 \leq y_1
    \end{cases} \right] \cdot
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{2} e^{2 - y_1 / 2}, & 4 \leq y_1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases}\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        (e^{2 - y_1 / 2})^2\\
        = e^{4 - y_1}, & 4 \leq y_1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
    E(Y_{(1)}) & = \int_{y_1 = 4}^{y_1 = \infty} y_1 \cdot e^{4 - y_1} dy_1\\
    & = -y_1 e^{4 - y_1} \biggr|_{y_1 = 4}^{y_1 = \infty} - \int_{y_1 = 4}^{y_1 = \infty} -e^{4 - y_1} dy_1\\
    & = \left(0 + (4)e^{4 - (4)} \right) - \left[e^{4 - y_1} \right]_{y_1 = 4}^{y_1 = \infty}\\
    & = 4 - \left(0 - e^{4 - (4)} \right) = 5
\end{align*}
$$
