a) Letting $F(y)$ be the common exponential distribution function mean $\beta$, then

$$
\begin{align*}
    F_{Y_{(1)}}(y_1) & = 1 - [1 - F(y_1)]^n\\
    & = 1 - \left[1 -
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y_1 \leq 0,\\
        1 - e^{-y_1 / \beta}, & 0 < y_1
    \end{cases} \right]^n\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y_1 \leq 0,\\
        1 - (e^{-y_1 / \beta})^n\\
        = 1 - e^{-y_1 / (\beta / n)}, & 0 < y_1.
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

This is exactly the distribution function for an exponential distribution with mean $\beta / n$.
