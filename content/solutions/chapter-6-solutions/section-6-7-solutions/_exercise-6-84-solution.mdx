Letting $F(y)$ be the Weibull distribution function,

$$
\begin{align*}
    F(y) & =
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y \leq 0,\\
        \int_{t = 0}^{t = y} \frac{1}{\alpha} mt^{m - 1} e^{-t^m / \alpha} dt\\
        = \int_{u = 0}^{u = y^m / \alpha} e^{-u} du\\
        = -e^{-u} \biggr|_{u = 0}^{u = y^m / \alpha}\\
        = -e^{-(y^m / \alpha)} - (-e^{-(0)})\\
        = 1 - e^{-y^m / \alpha}, & 0 < y.
    \end{cases}\\
    \implies F_{Y_{(1)}}(y_1) & = 1 - [1 - F(y_1)]^n\\
    & = 1 - \left[1 -
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y_1 \leq 0,\\
        1 - e^{-y_1^m / \alpha}, & 0 < y_1
    \end{cases} \right]^n\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y_1 \leq 0,\\
        1 - e^{-y^m / (\alpha / n)}, & 0 < y_1
    \end{cases}\\
    \implies f_{Y_{(1)}}(y_1) & =
    \begin{cases}
        0, & y_1 \leq 0,\\
        \frac{1}{(\alpha / n)} my^{m - 1} e^{-y^m / (\alpha / n)}, & 0 < y_1.
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$

Thus, we can see that $Y_{(1)}$ does, indeed, have a Weibull distribution with parameters $\frac{\alpha}{n}$ and $m$.
