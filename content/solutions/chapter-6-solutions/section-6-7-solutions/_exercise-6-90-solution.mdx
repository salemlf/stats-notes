a) We can begin by noting that we have $n = 4$ calls in $t = 2$ minutes.
With these values, the joint density of $W_{(1)}, W_{(2)}, W_{(3)}$, and $W_{(4)}$ will be

$$
f(w_1, w_2, w_3, w_4) =
\begin{cases}
    \frac{4!}{2^4}= \frac{24}{16} = \frac{3}{2}, & 0 \leq w_1 \leq w_2 \leq w_3 \leq w_4 \leq 2,\\
    0, & \text{elsewhere.}
\end{cases}
$$

To find the marginal density $f_{W_{(4)}}(w_4)$, we can integrate the joint density above with respect to $w_1, w_2,$ and $w_3$, then use that to find the desired probability:

$$
\begin{align*}
    & \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \int_{w_2 = 0}^{w_2 = w_3} \int_{w_1 = 0}^{w_1 = w_2} \frac{3}{2} dw_1 dw_2 dw_3\\
    & = \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \int_{w_2 = 0}^{w_2 = w_3} \frac{3}{2} w_1 \biggr|_{w_1 = 0}^{w_1 = w_2} dw_2 dw_3\\
    & = \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \int_{w_2 = 0}^{w_2 = w_3} \left(\frac{3}{2} (w_2) - \frac{3}{2} (0) \right) dw_2 dw_3\\
    & = \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \int_{w_2 = 0}^{w_2 = w_3} \frac{3}{2} w_2 dw_2 dw_3\\
    & = \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \frac{3}{4} w_2^2 \biggr|_{w_2 = 0}^{w_2 = w_3} dw_3\\
    & = \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \left(\frac{3}{4} (w_3)^2 - \frac{3}{4} (0)^2 \right) dw_3\\
    & = \int_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} \frac{3}{4} w_3^2 dw_3\\
    & = \frac{1}{4} w_3^3 \biggr|_{w_3 = 0}^{w_3 = w_4} = \frac{1}{4} (w_4)^3 - \frac{1}{4} (0)^3 = \frac{1}{4} w_4^3\\
    \implies f_{W_{(4)}}(w_4) & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{4} w_4^3, & 0 \leq w_4 \leq 2,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}\\
    \implies P(W_{(4)} \leq 1) & = \int_{w_4 = 0}^{w_4 = 1} \frac{1}{4} w_4^3 dw_4\\
    & = \frac{1}{16} w_4^4 \biggr|_{w_4 = 0}^{w_4 = 1}\\
    & = \frac{1}{16} (1)^4 - \frac{1}{16} (0)^4 = \frac{1}{16} = 0.0625.
\end{align*}
$$

Alternatively, consider the note that the given joint density function $f(w_1, w_2, w_3, w_4)$ is for an ordered sample of size $n$ drawn from a uniform distribution on $(0, t)$ specifically.
Then for any given call, the time at which the call occurs $W$ will have a uniform distribution, and, thus, a distribution function of

$$
F(w) =
\begin{cases}
    0, & w < 0,\\
    \frac{1}{t} w, & 0 \leq w \leq t,\\
    1, & t < w.
\end{cases}
$$

With $n = 4$ and $t = 2$, this implies

$$
\begin{align*}
    F_{W_{(4)}}(w_4) & = [F(w_4)]^4 =
    \begin{cases}
        0, & w_4 < 0,\\
        \frac{1}{2^4} w_4^4, & 0 \leq w_4 \leq 2,\\
        1, & 2 < w_4
    \end{cases}\\
    \implies P(W_{(4)} \leq 1) & = F_{W_{(4)}}(1) = \frac{1}{16}.
\end{align*}
$$

b)

$$
\begin{align*}
    E(W_{(4)}) & = \int_{w_4 = 0}^{w_4 = 2} w_4 \cdot \frac{1}{4} w_4^3 dw_4\\
    & = \frac{1}{20} w_4^5 \biggr|_{w_4 = 0}^{w_4 = 2}\\
    & = \frac{1}{20} (2)^5 - \frac{1}{20} (0)^5\\
    & = \frac{32}{20} = \frac{8}{5} = 1.6 \text{ minutes}
\end{align*}
$$
