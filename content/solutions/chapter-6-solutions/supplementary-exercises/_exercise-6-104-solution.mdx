Let $p_1(y_1)$ and $p_2(y_2)$ the probability functions for $Y_1$ and $Y_2$ respectively, with the same parameter $p$.

a) If $Y_1 = Y_2 = y$ is some fixed positive integer, then

$$
\begin{align*}
    P(Y_1 = Y_2 = y) & = P(Y_1 = y)P(Y_2 = y) \quad (\text{by independence})\\
    & = p_1(y)p_2(y)\\
    & = p(1 - p)^{y - 1} \cdot p(1 - p)^{y - 1}\\
    & = p^2 (1 - p)^{2y - 2}.
\end{align*}
$$

Note that $0 < 1 - p < 1 \implies 0 < (1 - p)^2 < 1$.
Thus, to find $P(Y_1 = Y_2)$, we will have to sum $P(Y_1 = Y_2 = y)$ over all positive integers $y$:

$$
\begin{align*}
    P(Y_1 = Y_2) & = \sum_{y = 1}^{\infty} P(Y_1 = Y_2 = y)\\
    & = \sum_{y = 1}^{\infty} p^2 (1 - p)^{2y - 2}\\
    & = \frac{p^2}{(1 - p)^2} \sum_{y = 1}^{\infty} [(1 - p)^2]^y\\
    & = \frac{p^2}{(1 - p)^2} \cdot \frac{(1 - p)^2}{1 - (1 - p)^2}\\
    & = \frac{p^2}{1 - (1 - p)^2} = \frac{p^2}{1 - q^2}.
\end{align*}
$$
