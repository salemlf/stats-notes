First note that $Y_1$ and $Y_2$ are both gamma distributed with $\alpha = \beta = 2$.
Then

$$
\begin{align*}
    m_U(t) & = E(e^{tU}) = E(e^{t((1 / 2) Y_1 + Y_2)})\\
    & = E(e^{tY_1 / 2}) E(e^{tY_2 / 2})\\
    & = m_{Y_1}(t / 2) m_{Y_2}(t / 2)\\
    & = (1 - 2(t / 2))^{-2} (1 - 2(t / 2))^{-2}\\
    & = (1 - t)^{-4}.
\end{align*}
$$

This is the moment-generating function for a gamma random variable with $\alpha = 4$ and $\beta = 1$, making the density function

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_U(u) & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{u^{4 - 1} e^{-u / 1}}{1^4 \Gamma(4)}, & u > 0,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere}
    \end{cases}\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        \frac{1}{6} u^3 e^{-u}, & u > 0,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$
