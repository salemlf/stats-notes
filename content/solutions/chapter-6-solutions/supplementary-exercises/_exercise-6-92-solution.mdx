By Theorem 6.3, $U = \frac{1}{2} Y_1 + \left(-\frac{3}{2} \right) Y_2$ is normally distributed with

$$
\begin{align*}
    \mu_U & = \frac{1}{2} \mu + \left(-\frac{3}{2} \right) \mu = -\mu\\
    \sigma_U^2 & = \left(\frac{1}{2} \right)^2 \sigma^2 + \left(-\frac{3}{2} \right)^2 \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{4} \sigma^2 + \frac{9}{4} \sigma^2 = \frac{5}{2} \sigma^2.
\end{align*}
$$

Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    f_U(u) & = \frac{1}{\sigma_U \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-(u - \mu_U)^2 / 2 \sigma_U^2}\\
    & = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5 / 2} \sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-(u - (-\mu))^2 / 2 (5 / 2) \sigma^2}\\
    & = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{5 \pi}} e^{-(u + \mu)^2 / 5 \sigma^2}.
\end{align*}
$$
