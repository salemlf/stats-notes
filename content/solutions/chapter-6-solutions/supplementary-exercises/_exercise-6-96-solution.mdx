First note that $P(Y_1 > Y_2) = P(Y_1 - Y_2 > 0)$.
The random variable $U = Y_1 - Y_2$ will be normally distributed with mean $5 - 4 = 1$ and variance $1 + 3 = 4$.
Thus,

$$
\begin{align*}
    P(Y_1 > Y_2) & = P(Y_1 - Y_2 > 0) = P(U > 0)\\
    & = \int_{u = 0}^{u = \infty} \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-(u - 1)^2 / 2 \cdot 4} du\\
    & \approx 0.691462461274.\\
    \text{or, alternatively, } P(U > 0) & = P \left(\frac{U - 1}{\sqrt{4}} > -\frac{1}{\sqrt{4}} \right)\\
    & = \int_{z = -1 / 2}^{z = \infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-z^2 / 2} dz\\
    & \approx 0.691462461274.
\end{align*}
$$
