Let things be defined as in the hint.
Then $X$ has density function

$$
f_X(x) =
\begin{cases}
    \frac{1}{1 - (-1)} = \frac{1}{2}, & -1 \leq x \leq 1,\\
    0, & \text{elsewhere.}
\end{cases}
$$

We can then use this to find the distribution function of $U = |X|$:

$$
\begin{align*}
    F_U(u) & = P(U \leq u) = P(|X| \leq u)\\
    & = P(-u \leq X \leq u)\\
    & =
    \begin{cases}
        0, & u < 0,\\
        \int_{x = -u}^{x = u} \frac{1}{2} dx\\
        = \frac{1}{2} x \big|_{x = -u}^{x = u}\\
        = \frac{1}{2} (u) - \frac{1}{2} (-u)\\
        = u, & 0 \leq u \leq 1,\\
        1, & 1 < u
    \end{cases}\\
    \implies f_U(u) & =
    \begin{cases}
        1, & 0 \leq u \leq 1,\\
        0, & \text{elsewhere.}
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
$$
